Wearable integration

Use the wearable you already own.

We connect through openwearables.io — an open-source, unified health-data API — so your protocol, sauna, and recovery sessions can log to whichever wearable you already wear. No new device. No data lock-in.

One API, every wearable.

openwearables.io is the unified health-data layer we read from and write to. Same shape of data, regardless of which device you wear. The integration is open-source and auditable; the codebase is on GitHub. Your wearable data stays on your device and inside your wearable's own account — we orchestrate the log, not the storage.

  • Open-source bridge. openwearables.io is published under an open licence. You can audit how the log is written.
  • Unified data model. HRV, sleep, recovery, activity, workouts — one shape across providers.
  • No new device. Bring whichever wearable you already own. Switch later without losing history.
  • Your data stays with you. Sessions log to your wearable's own journal. We don't aggregate across users.
  • Garmin HRV · sleep · workouts · body battery
  • Whoop recovery score · strain · sleep
  • Oura readiness · sleep stages · HRV
  • Amazfit activity · sleep · stress
  • Apple Watch all of the above · via Apple Health

Common questions

The five answers before you ask.

Is this legal?

lifelongpep is being built as a booking and orchestration layer, not as a pharmacy. The planned model is doctor-gated: doctors prescribe independently, and any prescription fulfillment would happen through appropriately licensed partners against a valid prescription.

Does this guarantee a GLP-1 prescription?

No. The planned consult is for medical judgement, readiness, and risk review. Sometimes the answer may be lifestyle changes, further testing, another intervention, or no prescription at all.

How does the wearable integration work?

We use the openwearables.io API to log planned protocol activity — and, when we add them, your sauna, ice bath, salt bath, cold plunge, and red light therapy sessions — into your Garmin, Whoop, Oura, Amazfit, or Apple Watch journal, the same way you would log a workout or a meal. The wearable data itself stays on your device and your wearable account. None of these are medical devices; this is a personal-tracking convenience, not a clinical input. Your doctor can ask you to share specific data at follow-up if they want to.

Can I cancel or get a refund?

Booking is not open yet, so no consult payments are being collected on this site. Cancellation, refund, and purchase terms will be posted before paid consult booking launches.

What about the broader stack — sauna, supplements?

The planned rollout starts with the waitlist and provider network for metabolic longevity and GLP-1 readiness, then recovery bookings and supplements. The same flow is intended to handle consult, purchase or booking, and wearable tracking.
