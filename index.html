Wearable integration

Use the wearable you already own.

We connect through openwearables.io — an open-source, unified health-data API — so your peptide, sauna, and recovery sessions log to whichever wearable you already wear. No new device. No data lock-in.

One API, every wearable.

openwearables.io is the unified health-data layer we read from and write to. Same shape of data, regardless of which device you wear. The integration is open-source and auditable; the codebase is on GitHub. Your wearable data stays on your device and inside your wearable's own account — we orchestrate the log, not the storage.

  • Open-source bridge. openwearables.io is published under an open licence. You can audit how the log is written.
  • Unified data model. HRV, sleep, recovery, activity, workouts — one shape across providers.
  • No new device. Bring whichever wearable you already own. Switch later without losing history.
  • Your data stays with you. Sessions log to your wearable's own journal. We don't aggregate across users.
  • Garmin HRV · sleep · workouts · body battery
  • Whoop recovery score · strain · sleep
  • Oura readiness · sleep stages · HRV
  • Amazfit activity · sleep · stress
  • Apple Watch all of the above · via Apple Health

Common questions

The five answers before you ask.

Is this legal?

Yes. The doctor prescribes independently under the National Medical Commission's 2023 RMP regulations. Where a prescription leads to a peptide purchase, dispensing is handled by licensed compounding pharmacies that fulfill against a valid prescription. lifelongpep is the booking and orchestration layer; we don't operate as a pharmacy ourselves.

What if the doctor doesn't prescribe peptides?

That's fine and often the correct answer. The consult covers your goals, history, and what's appropriate for you — sometimes that's lifestyle changes, sometimes it's a different intervention, sometimes peptides aren't a fit. You're paying for the doctor's judgement, not for a guaranteed prescription.

How does the wearable integration work?

We use the openwearables.io API to log your peptide consumption — and, when we add them, your sauna, ice bath, salt bath, cold plunge, and red light therapy sessions — into your Garmin, Whoop, Oura, Amazfit, or Apple Watch journal, the same way you would log a workout or a meal. The wearable data itself stays on your device and your wearable account. None of these are medical devices; this is a personal-tracking convenience, not a clinical input. Your doctor can ask you to share specific data at follow-up if they want to.

Can I cancel or get a refund?

Cancel before the consult and you're not charged. If the consult happens but you don't proceed to a peptide purchase, you're only out the consult fee. Peptide purchases have a separate return policy posted with each line item before you buy — pharmacy returns on prescription medications are limited by law.

What about the broader stack — sauna, supplements?

Peptides today. Recovery bookings (sauna, steam, salt baths, cold plunge, red light therapy) come next. Supplements after that. The same flow handles all of them — consult if it needs a doctor, book or buy if it doesn't, track on your wearable either way.
