Doctor conversation

Ask better questions before GLP-1.

The goal is not to pressure a prescription. The goal is to understand fit, risk, alternatives, and follow-up.

Use this as a conversation guide with a qualified doctor. It is not medical advice.

Am I clinically eligible?

Ask what criteria the doctor uses, what history matters, and what would make GLP-1 inappropriate.

What are the main risks for me?

Ask about contraindications, side effects, current medication, family history, pregnancy plans, and when to stop or seek help.

How do we protect muscle?

Ask about protein, resistance training, fatigue, injury constraints, strength tracking, and whether body composition should be monitored.

What happens if I stop?

Ask about maintenance, appetite return, weight regain risk, follow-up, and what habits need to be in place before discontinuation.

What are my alternatives?

Ask what non-drug options, nutrition changes, training changes, sleep work, or other interventions should be considered first.

How will follow-up work?

Ask when to check in, what to track, how dose changes are handled, and how side effects are reviewed.
